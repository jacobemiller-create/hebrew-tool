שְׁמַע

Learn to Read Any Hebrew Word

Hebrew is built on a beautifully simple system: every syllable is a letter paired with a vowel. Learn those pairings and you can decode any Hebrew word — even one you've never encountered before. No rote memorization required.

The Decoding Method
1
Identify the Letter
Hebrew has 22 consonants. Each one makes a single, consistent sound. Look at the letter and ask: "What sound does this make?"
2
Identify the Vowel
A vowel mark — called nikud — appears below or above the letter. Ask: "What sound does this vowel add?"
3
Blend & Read
Say the letter sound and immediately add the vowel sound. That blend is one syllable. Chain your syllables left to right to form the word.
